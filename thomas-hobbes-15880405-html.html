Thomas Hobbes was an English philosopher, considered one of the founders of modern political philosophy. Hobbes is best known for his 1651 book Leviathan, in which he outlined an influential formulation of the theory of social contracts. In addition to political philosophy, Hobbes contributed to many other fields, including history, jurisprudence, geometry, gas physics, theology and ethics, as well as philosophy in general.
