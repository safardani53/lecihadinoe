Man left unable to walk with horrifying flesh-eating bacteria after being bitten during vicious fami

A HORRIFYING flesh-eating bacteria had left a man unable to walk after it infected his leg during a family fight.

Donnie Adams, a resident of Tampa Bay, Florida, survived the encounter with the flesh-eating bacteria after quick-thinking doctors operated.

Adams was at a family gathering in February when a fight broke out between two members, per St. Petersburg NBC News outlet WFLA.

After getting in the middle of the two in an attempt to break up the altercation, he suffered a bite to his left leg.

In a desperate effort to mitigate negative health effects, Adams went to the hospital and got a tetanus shot along with some antibiotics.

However, the infection seemed to worsen as time went on.

"By the third day, my leg was very sore," he told WFLA. "I couldn’t walk, it was very warm and very painful."

Later, Adams was taken to the emergency room at HCA Florida Pasadena Hospital in St. Petersburg.

Dr. Fritz Brink, an osteopathic physician at HCA Florida, said that after observing Adams' leg, he knew he had to act fast.

"I looked at him, and I said to him that I need to take you to the operating room,” Brink recalled.

The doctor continued to explain that he found necrotizing fasciitis, a flesh-eating bacteria that destroys healthy tissue in the body.

It enters through a break in the skin, which was a bite in Adams' case and can originate from several different bacteria, per the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC).

“There’s a lot of really bad bacteria that live between our teeth in our gums in our mouth,” Brink told the news outlet, as he believed the bacteria could have come from the teeth of the person who bit Adams.

Brink had to perform surgery twice to remove additional infected tissue from Adams' thigh and managed to save the Tampa Bay resident's leg.

“If I would’ve waited … until the next day after that ER second visit, there was a good chance I would’ve lost my leg,” Adams said.

Had he waited an additional day to go in, the outcome could have also been fatal, as septic shock might have set in.

Brink explained that infections evolve rapidly, and any person with open wounds should watch for signs of it worsening and seek medical attention immediately.

Redness, increased pain, or warmth are all symptoms.

For Adams, the situation was horrific, but he is incredibly thankful.

“They assessed my wound and it was very horrific,” he said.

“It was unbelievable. But in my mind, I just had to get through whatever this was.”

“What you see now, you see not just a scar, but the beauty of the aftermath,” Adams continued.

“I would’ve never imagined that a human bite would turn into something so horrific as a flesh-eating bacteria.”
