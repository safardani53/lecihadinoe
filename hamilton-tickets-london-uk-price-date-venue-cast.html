HAMILTON has become one of the most successful Broadway musicals in history since opening in New York in 2015.

As soon as tickets for the UK shows became available they were snapped up by eager fans, here's how you can still see the critically-acclaimed production...

What is the story behind Hamilton: An American Musical?

The musical is based on the life of one of America’s Founding Fathers, Alexander Hamilton.

Predictably, it's not all singing and dancing: the plot is inspired by the real events documented in Ron Chernow's biography of the Founding Father.

Specifically, it covers his journey as an immigrant from the West Indies to become George Washington’s right hand-man during the Revolutionary War and the nation’s first Treasury Secretary.

The statesman would go on to establish America's first bank, and played a pivotal role in establishing the US as a free and independent country.

How successful has the musical been?

Created by New Yorker Lin-Manuel Miranda, the Broadway production received almost universally positive praise from critics, particularly for its diverse cast, its nods to theatre classics and its soundtrack.

The show has won the Pulitzer Prize for drama, been nominated for a record-breaking 16 Tony awards and spawned a Grammy award-winning soundtrack.

Marilyn Stasio, in her review of the Off-Broadway production for Variety, wrote: "The music is exhilarating, but the lyrics are the big surprise.

"The sense, as well as the sound of the sung dialogue, has been purposely suited to each character."

Writing in The New York Times, Ben Brantley said: "I am loath to tell people to mortgage their houses and lease their children to acquire tickets to a hit Broadway show.

"But Hamilton, directed by Thomas Kail and starring Mr. Miranda, might just about be worth it."

When did it come to London?

Hamilton was set to open at The Victoria Palace Theatre - just by London Victoria train station and built right on top of a giant active sewer - on November 1, 2017.

However, in September it was announced the show's opening was being put back a whole month due to problems with the theatre's renovation - and it opened on December 21.

Producer Jeffrey Seller told thousands of annoyed ticketholders: "We are extremely sorry to disappoint patrons who we know expended time, effort and valuable resources to purchase tickets for our first performances.

"They will be given immediate priority so that they can be re-seated as early as possible."

Hamilton will run right through until December 15, 2018, with performances on most days, apart from Sundays, during this run.

The regular show times feature 7.30pm performances Monday - Saturday, with additional 2.30pm performances on Thursdays and Saturdays.

Can I still get tickets for Hamilton?

Yes, after extending the hit musical until the end of 2018 - those who have not yet grabbed tickets still can.

The Hamilton website, Delfont Mackintosh and Ticketmaster are the only three places you can get tickets, with all three selling from the same allocation.

Details of pricing, the performance schedule and all future booking information will be available on the official website.

Who’s in the West End cast?

Recent graduate Jamael Westman will be making his West End debut in the titular role of Alexander Hamilton, with Ash Hunter filling in for him in certain performances.

Meanwhile, Olivier Award nominated actor Michael Jibson will step into King Georges' shoes for the West End run.

The part of Aaron Burr will be taken on by Giles Terera, who has also starred in The Book of Mormon and Avenue Q.

Rachel Ann Go will be taking on the role of Alexander Hamilton's wife Eliza Schuyler, she has previously appeared in Les Miserables and Miss Saigon.

The role of her sister, Angelica, will be taken on by former Bodyguard star Rachel John.

Obioma Ugoala will be portraying George Washington and Christine Allado will be Peggy Schuyler and Maria Reynolds.

Jason Pennycooke has been cast to play Lafayette and Thomas Jefferson and Cleve September is all set to play John Laurens and Philip Hamilton.

The characters of Hercules Mulligan and James Madison will be played by recent graduate Tarinn Callender.

The leading role of Alexander Hamilton was announced later than the other parts, leading to speculation that show creator Lin-Manuel Miranda would take on the role at some point during its West End run.
