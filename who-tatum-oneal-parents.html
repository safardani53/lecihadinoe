Who are Tatum O'Neal's parents?

PAPER MOON actress Tatum O'Neal became the youngest person ever to win an Academy Award back in 1974.

Fans of the 60-year-old Hollywood star are rarely surprised to hear that she grew up in a talent-filled household.

Who is Tatum O'Neal?

Tatum Beatrice O'Neal was born in Los Angeles, California, on November 5, 1963.

She is an actress best known for her role as Addie Loggins in the '70s film, Paper Moon.

Over the years, O'Neal has starred in the films The Bad News Bears (1976), Nickelodeon (1976), and Little Darlings (1980). She has also been featured in shows including Sex and the City, 8 Simple Rules, and Law & Order: Criminal Intent.

Her net worth is estimated to be around $1.5million, according to Celebrity Net Worth.

Who are Tatum O'Neal's parents?

O'Neal was born to Hollywood stars Ryan O'Neal and Joanna Moore.

The pair tied the knot in 1963 but went their separate ways after three years of marriage.

Ryan O'Neal

Charles Patrick Ryan O'Neal was born in Los Angeles, California, on April 20, 1941.

Ryan was an actor and former boxer known for his iconic role as Rodney Harrington on the ABC nighttime soap opera Peyton Place.

As an Academy Award and Golden Globe nominee, O'Neal was featured in films like Love Story (1970), What's Up, Doc? (1972), Paper Moon (1973), Barry Lyndon (1975), A Bridge Too Far (1977), and The Driver (1978).

He also played the recurring role of Max in the Fox television series, Bones, from 2005 to 2017.

He passed away on December 8, 2023, after a decades-long battle with cancer. He was 82.

Joanna Moore

Dorothy Joanne Cook - professionally known as Joanna Moore - was born in Parrott, Georgia, on November 10, 1934.

She was an actress known for being featured in several popular TV shows including The Fugitive, Bewitched, The Real McCoys, and The Andy Griffith Show from 1962 to 1963.

Moore passed away from lung cancer in November 1997 at the age of 63.

What happened to Tatum O'Neal in 2020?

On July 5, 2023, it was revealed that O'Neal previously suffered a severe stroke back in May 2020, which left her in a six-week coma.

"I almost died," she told PEOPLE in an exclusive interview.

O'Neal was found by a friend in her Century City apartment and rushed to a hospital after she overdosed on a combination of pain medication, opiates, and morphine.

"It was the phone call we'd always been waiting for," her eldest son, Kevin McEnroe, told PEOPLE.

"She also had a cardiac arrest and a number of seizures. There were times we didn't think she was going to survive." 

In the hospital, O'Neal was diagnosed with aphasia and has since fought to regain her full memory using daily therapy.
