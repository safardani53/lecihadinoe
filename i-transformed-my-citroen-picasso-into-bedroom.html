A BRIT woman has transformed her car into a bedroom to beat the cost of living crisis.

TikToker Zoe Bond turned to living in her car after sleeping on her mum's sofa for five months.

With a bit if work, she managed to transform her Citroen into a bedroom on wheels to make space for herself - and her little dog.

Sharing a video on TikTok, Zoe showed the entire transformation of her car.

She first removed the parcel tray from the back to free up her boot space.

Then she tumbled down the last row of seats in the motor before completely pulling them out of the car.

Just by removing he boot shelf and the seats, Zoe managed to make ample of space for herself.

She said in the video: "This is all the space...I can lie down perfectly."

In another video, Zoe transformed the space into a cozy room by putting the bedroom essentials.

She installed a layer of insulation on the floor of the car and then put a mattress made of memory foam.

The woman then covered it with soft bedsheets and quilt to make it more comfortable.

Zoe even covered her windows and other gaps with insulations to make the inside warm and toastie.

She now plans to live in the car with her dog to beat the unprecedented cost of living crisis.

Meanwhile, another man with a van can quickly turned his work vehicle into a campervan to explore the world.

Motorist and traveler Stephen Reid revealed in a TikTok video how converted his work van into a camper in just 10 minutes.

The 38-second TikTok video was posted by Stephen, who also posts his own YouTube videos about his outdoor adventures.

In the video, Stephen shows how he quickly makes the conversion and can fit all the things he needs for his holiday.

Firstly, he installs a custom-built bed made of railing from Ikea and plywood.

He then places a mattress, sheets, and quilting onto the plywood, making himself a bed, which can also be a couch.

Stephen then is able to add all the storage units he needs for his clothing, including a porta potty underneath the bed.

With the size of the van he also has enough room to store food and can even fit a small portable power station, an E-bike and a paddleboard.
