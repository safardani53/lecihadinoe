MANCHESTER UNITED are reportedly ready to listen to offers for several fringe players - and they are trying to offload Phil Jones.

The Red Devils are still on cloud nine after their dramatic late comeback to clinch victory over Man City in the Saturday’s derby.

They are currently fourth in the Premier League, a point behind their second-placed neighbours, as they prepare to face Crystal Palace tomorrow.

Cristiano Ronaldo’s controversial departure left them short up front.

But Erik ten Hag moved quickly this month to address that problem by bringing in Wout Weghorst on loan from Burnley.

Goalkeeper Jack Butland has also arrived from Wednesday’s opponent’s to provide back-up to David De Gea.

That has left boss Erik ten Hag looking to offload several men out of his bloated squad before the close of the window.

Footy Insider has now named three of the main ones that United are ready to listen to offers for.

And former England international defender Jones, 30, is one of those.

It’s the latest blow for hapless Jones, who along with De Gea is United’s longest-serving player, and another twist in his bizarre career that had hit the buffers even before he signed a four-year £110,000-week contract in February 2019.
