Who is Speedo Mick and how much money has he raised for charity? The Sun

HE'S braved gusts of more than 90 miles per hour and been pelted with rain, but nearly-naked 'Speedo Mick' is determined to raise loads of money "in me bloomers".

So why has the Everton fan walked "close to a marathon a day"?

Who is Speedo Mick?

Michael Cullen, 55, is becoming famous across Britain as 'Speedo Mick'.

Clad in his blue Speedos, socks, shoes and little else, on December 6 the Everton superfan embarked on an epic walk from John O'Groats in Scotland to reach Land's End in Cornwall, on February 22.

The Liverpudlian explains on his GoFundMe page: "It will take at least seven weeks and I will be walking close to a marathon a day.

"I will of course be making this journey whilst being in my swimming attire, ie Speedos, goggles and hat which will make this challenge a very cold one - but it will bring a lot of smiles to people’s faces."

According to charity Leave The Light On - which helps adults and kids gain confidence and self-esteem - Speedo Mick was for "a long time in need of help.

"I have been the homeless person you see in the shop doorway, the addict, the alcoholic, the hopeless case that had made so many bad decisions that there was surely no way back.

"I had become isolated from society and left to my own devices... I had to accept help from others."

Because of his own personal battles, and to help others, he decided to trek 1,000 miles to raise money for disadvantaged young people in need of support to "overcome isolation".

When asked how he was coping during Storm Ciara, he replied: "Speedos are designed to get wet and mine are absolutely soaking in this weather.

"The wind is whipping my back, and it feels like I am getting flogged.

"I really wouldn't advise anybody else to do this - I can't believe I am out in this myself.

"But, I am doing this for a really good cause and no storm is going to stop me getting to Land's End to complete this challenge."

How much money has he raised for charity?

Despite being clad "in me shreddies" and trudging through wintry weather – including a bone-chilling -6C – during Storms Ciara and Dennis, Speedo Mick has surpassed his £250,000 target.

He has thanked people for their "amazing support" along the route, and for donating an impressive £304,000, as of midday on February 22.
