Ray Bolger (Movie Actor) - Age, Birthday, Bio, Facts, Family, Net Worth, Height &amp; More

Raymond Wallace Bolger is an American film and television actor, Vaudeville, singer, dancer and theatrical performer who started in the era of silent film. He was a great Broadway artist in the 1930s and beyond. He is known for his roles as Scarecrow and Kansas counterpart "Hunk" in The Wizard of Oz and the villain Barnaby in Walt Disney's fantasy musical Babes in Toyland. He was also the host of his TV show of the same name, The Ray Bolger Show 1953 and 1955, originally Where's Raymond?
