Is Taylor Swift Attending Travis Kelces Next Kansas City Chiefs Game? Hollywood Life

Taylor Swift has already attended several of Travis Kelce‘s Kansas City Chiefs games since September, but will she be at the next one at their home stadium? The Chiefs are set to play against the Philadelphia Eagles, who Travis’ brother Jason Kelce plays for, at their Arrowhead Stadium in Kansas City, Missouri on Monday, November 20 — but don’t expect to see the popstar in the bleachers.

Taylor was reportedly planning on going and cheering on her beau, according to Entertainment Tonight. Unfortunately, she will not be able to make it, as she’s performing a concert of her Eras Tour in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, after postponing her show on Saturday, November 18. The show at Estadio Nilton Santos was postponed due to “extreme heat” in Brazil after a fan died from cardiac arrest before the concert on Friday.

While the “Love Story” singer may not be at the game, it was reported that her parents would attend the game and meet Travis’ parents for the first time, according to Entertainment Tonight. It’s unclear if Taylor missing the game will affect whether or not her parents attend.

Taylor made her return to the stage on November 9, when her tour went down to South America. Travis attended one of her concerts in Argentina, and the singer even gave him a shout-out from the stage during “Karma.” The pair was spotted kissing backstage after the show. She’ll be touring on and off over the next couple of months in international places like Brazil, Japan, Sweden, Australia, Canada, France, Italy and more. Ahead of the shows, an insider told Entertainment Tonight that Travis plans on attending some of the “Midnight Rain” singer’s international shows when his schedule allows so they won’t be away from each other for too long.

“Travis and Taylor are all in. The two are very into each other and are enjoying their time together but are also planning for the future. Taylor starts her international tour in November, and Travis is planning to be there to spend time with her,” the source said.  “Travis and Taylor are very serious about their careers, and the two bond over that and want to show support for each other whenever they can.”

Travis first attended The Eras Tour in Kansas City back in July. Shortly after the show, he admitted he tried to give a friendship bracelet to Taylor with his phone number on it, but was unsuccessful. He then later revealed he invited her to one of his Chiefs games and she ended up showing up, instantly sparking serious romance rumors. Since then, the lovebirds have been seen out and about on dinner dates and at star-studded events, like a Saturday Night Live afterparty in New York City, NY.

As their relationship has heated up, they haven’t been shy about showing off PDA during their latest outings. Taylor also posed while kissing Travis’ cheek after the Chiefs game against the Chargers on October 22, in someone’s social media photo. The tight end gushed about Taylor in a WSJ Magazine interview, published on November 20.
