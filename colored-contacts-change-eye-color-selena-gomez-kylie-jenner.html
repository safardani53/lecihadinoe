Makeup artist Allan Avendano, who works with stars like Sarah Hyland, Gigi Hadid, Zendaya and many more, is breaking down how to pick the right makeup while wearing colored contacts. 

AIR OPTIX COLORS contact lenses come in nine natural-looking colors. There are 5 more subtle, everyday colors — Pure Hazel, Blue, Green, Gray and Brown.

If you really want to amp up your look, try a more vibrant shade like Brilliant Blue, Gemstone Green, Honey or Sterling Gray.

This is a totally new way to change up your look!

Colored Contacts — How To Wear Beauty Trend Like Selena Gomez

Allan is explaining what makeup shades go best with your new eye color!

“Sterling Gray and Gray:

Cooler tones such as navy or dark gray add a great contrast, and similarly, dark brown shades or smoky tones also do a good job in bringing out gray eyes.

Gemstone Green and Green:

Purple or a deep plum shadow can really bring out the hues in green eyes and make them look instantly lighter and brighter.”

“Brilliant Blue and Blue:

You really want to stick to colors that are more neutral such as grays or browns, as they tend to complement the blue eye color instead of distracting from it.

Pure Hazel, Honey and Brown:

Honey and Brown tones have a wide range of color options when it comes to eye shadow. Having a darker eye-color, and since brown is already neutral, you can use any hue to make them pop. My favorite is adding peach or purple tones.”
