Cheltenham 2018 dress code: Here's what you should wear at the festival

YOU'VE got your ticket, researched the runners and riders - and are all set for a day out at Cheltenham.

This year's Festival promises to be as good as ever, with four days of racing set to wow a bumper crowd in the Gloucestershire countryside.

But if you're heading to the racecourse, what should you be wearing? SunSport has got you covered.

What is the dress code for Cheltenham Festival?

AT Cheltenham, the dress code is more lenient than say Royal Ascot - although if you want to wear a top hat you're more than welcome.

Throughout the four days, it's effectively dress to impress.

Wear what you're most comfortable in, but most people tend to make a real effort to smarten up.

It also depends what enclosure you're in.

Men usually wear a suit or similar in the Club Enclosure and in restaurants and hospitality facilities, whilst the women often don fantastic hats throughout the Festival.

Fancy dress IS permitted - but the racecourse do reserve the right to refuse entry if you've gone a little over the top.

Bear in mind that if you are going on Gold Cup Day and are thinking of wearing a Fred Flintstone outfit in the Club Enclosure, you will not be able to get in.

Fancy dress isn't permitted in that enclosure on the final day.

When is Cheltenham Festival 2018?

THIS years meet takes place between Tuesday 13 to Friday 16 March, 2018. 

The opening races take place on Champion Day, Wednesday is Ladies Day, Thursday is St Patricks Day before the meet concludes on Friday, with Gold Cup Day.

What channel can I watch it on?

YOU will be able to watch every minute of the Festival live on ITV Racing.

Coverage will run throughout each of the four days, starting in the morning and running through to the final races at 5.30
