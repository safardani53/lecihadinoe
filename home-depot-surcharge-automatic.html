Home Depot adds automatic surcharge and customers might not realize the added fees until after they

CUSTOMERS who return items to Home Depot by mail will have to pay for shipping - unless they can prove the return is due to a store mistake.

Home Depot customers can return items bought online to a physical store at no extra cost.

Yet, returning an online item by mail means paying any shipping costs.

The one exception is when customers are returning an item due to a confirmed error by Home Depot.

Most items delivered by UPS or FedEx will include a small parcel return label, yet customers can send items back without it so long as the return address on the package is correct.

Customers will receive a store credit once the item is received at the Home Depot warehouse.

The return rules are slightly different for customers in Hawaii and Alaska.

Those buyers must return non-UPS items at a store.

It may take two to 10 days for the return credit to be applied to a customer's store account.

For more information, check out Home Depot's online return policy guide.

MORE WAYS TO SAVE

In addition to watching out for hidden costs like return shipping fees, there are various ways to save at Home Depot and other retailers.

Walmart shoppers often find that the biggest in-store sales are hidden.

This means you have to scan the item's barcode with your Walmart app to reveal its markdown.

And some of these deals can be massive.

A TikTok influencer bought a nearly $200 dirt bike for just $19 thanks to a hidden clearance sale.

Another was able to find an entire patio set on clearance for just $5.

The price was originally $897.

There are also apps that can offer consistent savings on everything from high-end appliances to food.

These include Ibotta, which gives customers cash-back rewards for items it is currently promoting.

Many of the most successful couponers will also combine Ibotta rewards with others from other apps like Shopkick and Coupons.com to turbocharger savings.
