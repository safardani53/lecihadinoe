Scott Disick one-ups the Kardashians' over-the-top Christmas decor with massive Hanukkah sign inside

SCOTT Disick has joined the not-so-friendly competition among the Kardashian kids for most outrageous holiday decorations.

Kourtney's ex showed off a giant Hanukkah banner that nearly stretched across the entire length of his living room.

Scott got in the festive spirit Thursday, which was the first night of Hanukkah.

The Jewish festival of lights will stretch over the next eight days, or nearly as long as Scott's massive sign.

The 40-year-old took to Instagram to show off his Hanukkah haul.

Hanging above his multiple couches was a series of inflated blue letter balloons, arranged to spell HAPPY HANUKKAH, along with an exclamation point for emphasis.

In the caption, the Talentless designer shared: "HAPPY HANUKKAH MY JEWS!!!"

KEEPING UP

Scott's contribution comes amid a festive family feud among the Kardashian sisters.

His ex, Kourtney, even one-upped her sister Kim's decor with her latest festive display, fans insisted.

Kourtney showed off an incredible choir scene she'd created out of elf-on-the-shelf dolls and fruit in the kitchen of her $9 million home.

She posted a video of an elf-on-the-shelf doll sitting on an upturned mug serving as conductor to a choir.

There was a tiny book of music notes open in front of him, glued to the end of a wooden stick

The "choir" consisted of more elf-on-the-shelf carolers as well as tangerines with faces drawn on them in black marker.

Leaning against a wooden table was a cardboard ramp painted red with the words "elf sledding now open" written on the top.

Kourtney attached two elf-on-the-shelf dolls to little paper plates and taped them to the board to look like they were sledding down the slope.

The mother of four added little whisps of white cotton to look like snow for decoration.

One baby elf was bundled in a blanket and covered in tinsel as it joined the adult elves on the make-believe sled.

Kourtney captioned her post: "Elf season has begun."

KIM'S CORNER

While the Lemme founder has gone all out for the festive season, Kim, 43, has been decidedly more restrained with her decor.

The Kardashians star showed off the tiny lights that festooned the driveway leading up to her $60 million home in Hidden Hills last weekend.

She shared a video of the trees all along the path lit up with silver lights strung around the trunk and leaves.

The black security wall separated to reveal dozens of trees inside her front yard also illuminated with glimmering lights.

Critics slammed the decorations in an online thread.

"It so confusing with all the light, and then compared the house looks like the entrance to a maximum security prison," one wrote.

"How can you let a billion lights look so boring and pale? She just never disappoints," another added.

"This reminds me of an Apple Store for some reason," a third posted.
