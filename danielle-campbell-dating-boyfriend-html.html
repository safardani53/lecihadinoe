Know about 'All American' Star Danielle Campbell's Movies, Age, Dating Status, Height &amp; Net Worth

You may remember American actress Danielle Marie Campbell as Jessica Olson in the 2010 Disney movie Starstruck, Simone Daniels in the 2011 Disney film Prom, Davina Claire in the 2013 CW TV drama The Originals, and Kayla Powell and Olivia Moon in the 2018 CBS All Access psychological thriller series Tell Me a Story.

But since her 2020's appearance on Tell Me a Story, the Hinsdale native hasn't appeared in any projects yet.

Her last movie appearance was Being Frank, in which she portrayed the character, Allison, in 2019  

Read the full article to know about Danielle Campbell's dating & marital status, height, age, and net worth.

Does Danielle Campbell have a Married or a Husband?

Campbell, who stands at the height of 5 feet and 3 inches (1.6m), hasn't been married yet. However, she has been dating actor Colin Woodell for a long time now. 

And she and her longtime boyfriend, Colin Woodell, are nothing short of the couple's goals. 

The adorable pair have been seen together since 2014, and they have grown their friendship and relationship hand-in-hand.

The year, the pair went to Thailand for a trip.

The Tell Me a Story actress was previously dating One Direction singer Louis Tomlinson from late 2015 to early 2017.

Danielle Campbell and Louis Tomlinson in April 2016 (Photo: Instagram)

Campbell and Tomlinson first met in November 2015, when the Englishman was expecting his first child, Freddie, with ex-girlfriend Briana Jungwirth.

How Old is Danielle Campbell?

Born on 30 January 1995 (age 28) in Hinsdale, Illinois, U.S. to her real estate fund manager parents Georganne and John Campbell, Danielle has a younger brother. 

Danielle was first discovered by an agency representative at the age of 10 when she was doing her hair at a hair salon 

"I kind of fell into acting. When we moved back to the States, I was getting my hair done with my mom in the city here in Chicago, and somebody approached my mom and asked me to audition for their (casting) agency. And in my first week I booked a national Build-A-Bear Workshop commercial," she revealed to Chicago Tribune.

Her mom told the magazine, "She was always quite the little actress in my opinion, and always liked performing."

In 2006, she landed her first on-screen stint as Gracey Hollander in the American television drama series, Prison Break.
