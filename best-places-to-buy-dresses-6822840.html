The 17 Best Places to Buy Dresses of 2024

No matter the occasion, shopping for dresses can get a bit overwhelming. If you want a new comfy dress you will ultimately live in, need to find that perfect wedding guest dress, or are looking for workwear dresses that are office appropriate yet still feel like you (and can moonlight as going out dresses for happy hour during the week), knowing where to start in your shopping search is key.

As far as the best places to buy dresses is concerned, Naz Meknat, a celebrity stylist who works with stars like Sterling K. Brown, says that “it all depends on what kind of dress you are looking for, the occasion, your budget, and your style.” She recommends starting at a department store or multi-brand online retailer for more variety and “access to different designers, a variety of colors, sizes, and prices all in one place.”

Whether you prefer the variety of a department store or are looking for a more curated dress collection to shop from, we researched dozens of retailers, carefully considering styles, size offerings, shipping and return policies, and more to find the ones with the best dresses. As a result, we put together a list of our top picks for all of your dress-shopping needs.

Keep reading to discover the best places to buy dresses this spring and summer.

Best Overall

Amazon

Who It's Good For Who It's Not Good For

Nothing says variety and affordability quite like Amazon, which is home to thousands of dresses from hundreds of brands. No matter what you’re looking for, you’ll most likely find it here, making the retail giant the very best place to buy dresses, whether you're on the hunt for designer options or budget-friendly buys.

We personally love the affordable wedding guest dresses available, like this floral maxi dress that comes in a whopping 28 colors, as well as plenty of casual options (including the Amazon Essentials T-shirt Dress) for any sunny daytime occasion that can even be worn with flip flops. In terms of designer brands, you can find a wonderful curation at The Shop by Shopbop. For example, this white poplin dress by Rails could easily be dressed up or down depending on the occasion, which only adds to its value (plus, it's currently on sale).

In addition to variety, many dresses on Amazon are included in the retailer’s Try Before You Buy initiative, allowing you to order and try on at home before purchasing. Prime Members can receive free one- to two-day shipping on a majority of dress styles, as well.

Size Range: Varies, depending on the brand | Shipping Policy: Free Prime Shipping for members | Return Policy: Free returns for most Prime items

Best Return Policy

Nordstrom

Who It's Good For

  • A wide variety of labels, price points, size ranges, as well as free shipping and a lenient return policy will make you fall in love with shopping here.

Who It's Not Good For

Per Meknat’s advice, one of our top recommended department stores is the best place for shopping tons of variety in designers, styles, prices, and sizes. Nordstrom is home to everything from runway designers to smaller brands and everything in between, offering a collection of dress styles that work for all occasions, including casual everyday looks, formal attire, workwear dresses, and even bridal style.

Size Range: XXS-4XL, plus petite and maternity | Shipping Policy: Free shipping, no minimum | Return Policy: Generous case-by-case basis

Best Value

Target

Target's offerings are not only relatively affordable, but the quality is there too, thanks to in-house brands like A New Day, Knox Rose, and Wild Fable to trend-forward partnerships with Who What Wear. Target also drops Designer Collections in the spring and fall, launching collaborations with both up-and-coming and seasoned labels. The latest spring collection featured designs from Fe Noel, Rhode, and Agua Bendita, while past collections include the likes of Missoni, Rodarte, Zac Posen, Jason Wu, and Lilly Pulitzer. These collections mean you can get access to your favorite designer labels — only at a Target price point. We can't imagine anything better.

Plus, Target offers plenty of discounts and sales, especially for those signed up for Target Circle, the retailer's reward program. Look for end-of-season sales to get the biggest discounts on dresses. Aside from the designer collections, most of Target's dress offerings lean casual — however they still offer styles appropriate for outings like weekend brunch and summer wedding attire.

Size Range: XXS-4XL | Shipping Policy: Free shipping on orders $35+ and any orders paid with RedCard; same-day shipping on select items for $10 with Shipt | Return Policy: Return within 90 days for full refund on most items (120 days if purchased with RedCard); free return shipping when mailed

How to Pick the Right Place to Buy Dresses Online

Size Range

First and foremost, you want to make sure a dress is available in your size, as there is nothing worse than falling in love with a dress only to find out your size is sold out or unavailable. If you do end up falling loving a dress that has your size but is sold out, many brands and companies offer restock notifications, which can ultimately help you snag the dress of your dreams once it becomes available again. Another hack: Nordstrom offers free tailoring on anything — whether or not it was purchased from the retailer — so you can always size up and get it tailored in-store if you need to.

Price Range 

It’s also important to consider your budget as well as look at the price range of dresses offered when shopping for dresses. If you plan to invest in a dress with a higher price tag, Meknat says to “make sure it’s not too trendy and won’t go out of style by the next season if you want to invest in a piece that can be worn more than once.” That's why we picked Reformation as a splurge option — the brand's silhouettes have timeless appeal, and the high-quality materials increase the longevity of the dresses.

It’s also important to have things like your personal style and the occasion dialed in before considering your budget. “It’s better to have a clear vision of what you’re looking for first and then go to the stores and brands within your budget rather than the other way around,” says Elysha Lenkin, a TV and celebrity stylist who has worked with Carrie Underwood, Serena Williams, and Tina Fey.

Shipping and Return Policies

Before clicking add to cart, be sure to double-check the shipping and return policies. Not only do you want to have a clear idea of the policies and how soon your dress will arrive, but you also want to make sure your dress is returnable if you’re unsure of the sizing. Many retailers offer returns on most items, though some sale items could be final-sale, depending on the store. Nordstrom, Walmart, and Amazon are best in class when it comes to returns — Amazon and Walmart have free shipping and returns for members, and Nordstrom has such a generous return policy that usually takes any returns without a time limit, even without a receipt or the original packaging.

Fit

“It’s also important to consider your body type while shopping for a dress, as different styles complement different body types,” says Meknat. She recommends getting to know your body shape and which styles are “the most flattering to your shape and size” before honing in on a dress type.

Comfort is another key fit component. “I know that isn’t necessarily everyone’s point of view, but I think there is a way to combine comfort with looking your absolute best,” says Daniela Romero, a celebrity stylist who has worked with Sarah Hyland and Kaley Cuoco. Romero also recommends trying something new. If it doesn’t fit right, she recommends a quick trip to the tailor to achieve that amazing fit and feel.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Which brand is best for dresses?

    We picked Amazon as the best overall place to shop for dresses due to the retailer's wide variety of styles, price points, designers, and sizes, as well as plenty of clearance items. The online retailer is also known for its generous return policy as well as its Try Before You Buy program.

  • Are expensive dresses worth it?

    It totally depends on your budget and the item's potential cost-per-wear, but overall, investing in a dress that's versatile and high-quality can be worth it. If you can find a timeless, well-made black dress or that special red dress that can accompany you to formals, wedding events, dinner dates, office parties, and the like, it can be worth investing in. Cheaper dresses don't always last long and tend to be more trend-forward, meaning you'll get less wear out of them in the long run.

  • Is a cocktail dress long or short?

    According to Lenkin, a cocktail dress can be whatever length you choose (though they do tend to be on the shorter side). “Focus on the styles you love and the length that suits your body best,” she advises. “The key is to feel comfortable so that you’re not tugging at a shorter hemline or tripping over too much fabric around your ankles,” she adds. For a more modern spin on a cocktail dress, Lenkin recommends a classy floral maxi dress in a silk chiffon fabric.

  • What makes a dress office-appropriate?

    “A perfect dress to wear to the office is anything that isn’t too short, so you can sit comfortably in it,” says Romero. “I think you should be able to show your personality in office clothes and have some fun with it, though." To do so, Romero recommends looking at colors, patterns, and different necklines to mix it up. “It comes down to the accessories for executing a look and making it look super-polished,” she adds.

  • What is the difference between cocktail and formal attire?

    “It used to be that cocktail attire was shorter, and formal attire was longer, but that is no longer the case,” says Lenkin. She says a short dress can be the perfect ensemble for a formal event, just like Reese Witherspoon once demonstrated on the Golden Globe Awards red carpet in her knee-length, bright yellow Nina Ricci dress. “Rather than get your guidance from the ‘formal’ or ‘cocktail’ descriptors, plug into other adjectives that resonate with the way you want to feel at the event, and then align your dress to express these words,” Lenkin recommends. “An elegant, sophisticated, or glamorous dress will work well for many occasions.”
