Americans can get free money from $9million 'dude' settlement - see if you're eligible

USERS of disposable wipes, sold at stores including Walmart, could get free money thanks to a $9million settlement.

The settlement came after customers said men's hygiene company Dude Products falsely advertised its body wipes as flushable.

The company denies any wrongdoing, but agreed to settle in a case impacting consumers who bought the product over an eight-year span.

Plaintiffs in the class-action suit claim the wipes are not flush-safe and may damage plumbing - a real problem for a company that hopes to replace toilet paper.

The company, which creates scented wipes for men, is known for its appearances on reality television show Shark Tank in 2015 and 2017.

HOW TO CLAIM YOUR CASH

Anyone who purchased Dude's "flushable" wipes between February 5, 2015 and August 8, 2023 can file a claim to get free cash, according to Top Class Actions.

Proof of purchase is not required.

Time is limited, however. Claims must be filed by November 11, 2023.

Consumers who file valid claims with proof of purchase will receive $20, while those without proof will get $2.50.

Those looking to file a claim can register through the settlement website to file.

DUDE DENIES WRONGDOING

While the plaintiffs claim that the wipes can damage plumbing, Dude Products maintains that there was no wrongdoing.

The company says on its website the products passed a industry tests for flushability.

Dude Wipes are made from "short, plant-based fibers that are designed to dissolve in water, similar to toilet paper," the company said.

The company still advertises its wipes as flushable.

"Rather than throwing in the towel (or wipe, rather) we made it our mission to revolutionize the personal care industry with a wipe thatâ€™s nice to your butt, your plumbing system and the environment," the company said in June 2023.

MORE FREE MONEY

Dude Products isn't the only company settling a class-action suit over false advertising.

Fruit giant Dole agreed to a $4.3 million settlement after customers complained that fruit was stored in "100 percent juice."

Dole denied any wrongdoing in the case.

The company will pay between $9 or $18 to customers who file before September 25.

Some other ongoing settlements offer larger payouts. Life insurance company Ethos Technologies will pay out $1million after a security breach released consumers' data.

Customers who file by December 20, 2023 could be paid up to $5,200.
