Monique Angela Hicks, known by her stage name Mo'Nique, is an American comedian and actress. She was first recognized for her role in a comedy monologue, making her debut as a member of The Queens of Comedy. In 2002, she received a Grammy Award nomination for Best Comedy Album. Mo'Nique began moving into mainstream cinema and television with the lead role Nicole "Nikki" Parker in the UPN series The Parkers from 1999 to 2004, as well as starring in films like Phat Girlz in 2006 and Welcome Home Roscoe Jenkins in 2008. In 2009, she was critically acclaimed for her performance in Precious, which she won many awards, including the Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress. , becomes the fourth African-American woman to win the award. She hosted The Mo'Nique Show from 2009 to 2011 and played Ma Rainey in the 2015 HBO biopic film Bessie, which she won a Primetime Emmy nomination for.
