Dolly Parton ready to pose for Playboy at 75 more than four decades after cover debut The US Sun

DOLLY Parton wants to pose for Playboy again - more than four decades after first appearing on the cover.

The singer - who made her Playboy debut on the magazine's October 1978 cover - revealed she wants to celebrate her upcoming 75th birthday by gracing the mag one final time.

At the time, Dolly was the first country star appear on magazine's cover, and was dressed in the iconic Playboy bunny outfit.

She recently admitted she'd love to return - four decades after her cover debut.

The singer told 60 Minutes Australia: "I don’t plan to retire. I just turned 74 and I plan to be on the cover of Playboy magazine again.

"See, I did Playboy magazine years ago and I thought it’d be such a hoot - if they’ll go for it, I don't know if they will - if I could be on the cover again when I’m 75."

When asked if she'd wear the same outfit, the performer added: "Maybe! I could probably use it, boobs are still the same."

Dolly recently made headlines after starting her own social media trend.

The craze involved sharing four photos of yourself that represent your LinkedIn, Facebook, Instagram and Tinder.

Dolly was one of the first stars to get on board.
