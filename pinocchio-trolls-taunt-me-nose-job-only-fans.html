I was branded Pinocchio and a witch for years - now cruel trolls taunt me over my new nose

A WOMAN cruelly mocked over her nose faced a fresh wave of trolling after getting cosmetic surgery to correct it.

Hayley Webb, 20, from Howlong, New South Wales, Australia, grew up with the nickname Pinocchio because of her nose.

So she decided to fix her insecurity and bag herself the flat stomach and bum she'd always dreamed of, at the age of 19.

Falling into a rut of bad mental health, Hayley finally forked out just over £22K ($42,000 AUD) from her TikTok and OnlyFans earnings for a nose job and a Brazilian butt lift - the latter seeing fat from her stomach transferred to her behind.

But she's now being trolled for her new look, with cruel social media commentators claiming she 'looks worse'.

"My whole life I've been called names for my nose, such as Pinocchio, or told I have a witch's nose," she said.

"I was constantly asked how many fingers I could fit up my nose.

"I truly got my dad's side of the family's nose, while my other siblings were blessed with my mum's nose.

"I always told myself I would save for a nose job and get it done before I turned 40 because I thought I could never afford it."

Little did Hayley know she'd be able to get one done at 19.

Hayley struggled with her mental health after losing her job during the pandemic.

She recalled: "I lost my full-time job and animal traineeship because of Covid.

"I was also broken up with, and I fell into a deep depression as a result.

"I was diagnosed with three mental disorders, and I was so broke.

"I lost 10kg and never left my bed. I didn't work for eight months and was making constant trips to the hospital for my mental health.

"The one and only time I have ever seen my dad cry was because he hated seeing me in the state I was in."

In these dark moment, Hayley found an unexpected outlet.

"I started OnlyFans for a couple of extra bucks, and I ended up blowing up on TikTok and OF.

"That couple of hundred bucks I wanted turned into thousands, which has now turned into a couple of hundred thousands.

"I would fix what I hated about myself over a new car or a house any day.

"So when I was 18, I researched BBLs and nose jobs and other surgeries and cosmetics.

"I saw a TikTok about BBLs. I had never looked into it before, but I got a quote for it and realised I could easily afford it, so I booked in the next day.

"I didn't even think about it, because I just wanted my stubborn fat gone.

"I got three rounds of filler then booked in my nose and BBL on the same day.

"After I turned 19, I travelled interstate for my BBL to get rid of my insecurity about not having a flat stomach and an hourglass figure.

Hayley waited roughly three months for both procedures.

"My doctor said no more surgeries for six weeks afterwards.

"As soon as that six-week mark hit, I had my nose job.

"I had a long nose - not fat or thick. As my surgeon said, 'Very bone structured, pointy and long.'

"My parents wish I had loved myself for the way I looked before.

"But neither my mother nor father disowned me for doing porn as they'd rather see me happy than rotting away in my bed for months on end."

Posting her post-op results online, with a newfound love for her body image, a lot of people were quick to voice their cruel remarks to Hayley, who is thrilled with her new look.

She adds: "I posted the results online because I love my outcome. I am so confident in myself and couldn't be happier.

"Every time I post something, I gain more followers and make more money on my site, even though I always get hate too.

"It doesn't affect me. The world is cruel, and the only opinion that matters is mine.

"I’m finally confident in myself. I don't care about what people that live thousands of miles away from me think.

"All the comments I got about my surgeries were 'looks the same', 'get a refund' or, 'looks worse', but I don't care.

"I just think, 'They didn't know me before and photos can never do justice for how someone looks in-person.'

"My surgeon specifically told me, 'There's only so much you can do to a nose - you will always have a larger nose than most.'

"Something I will never forget my surgeon saying was, 'We want to make your nose look normal so that it's not an insecurity someone can pick you on for' and that's exactly what he did.

"If you met me and I didn't mention that I've had a nose job, you wouldn't comment on it, because you wouldn't notice anything wrong.

"But if you met me pre-nose job, you would instantly think, 'Gosh, her nose is huge,' so, to me, it's now perfect.

"My parents say they don't even recognise me, but they are glad I’m happy and they made me promise to get no more surgery at such a young age.
