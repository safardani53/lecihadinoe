Prince Harry is no fun since marrying Meghan Markle, pal James Haskell reveals

PRINCE Harry is "no fun" since marrying Meghan Markle, according to friend James Haskell.

The former England rugby star hinted at how much the Duke of Sussex had changed since tying the knot with Meg at St George's Chapel at Windsor Castle in 2018.

The pair wed in a romantic ceremony in front of millions after a whirlwind romance - joining the Firm for royal duties.

But the couple then dramatically quit the Royal Family - moving to the US to start a new life with their son Archie, now 4, and his little sister Lilibet, 2.

The couple have had a busy few years since, buying a Montecito mansion while also releasing a Netflix documentary and launching their Archewell Foundation - while Prince Harry also released his biography, Spare.

But rugby legend James Haskell has now revealed how Harry has changed in the past few years.

Harry and James first met at Mike and Zara Tindall's wedding in 2011.

And speaking at the Bath Forum, in Somerset, for the World Cup Afterparty: The Good, The Bad and The Ugly, Haskwell joked: "Who should be there, wandering around having a good time, but Prince Harry.

"He hadn't met her [Meghan] yet, so he was still quite fun."

He also shared a number of quips about his mate, Mark Tindall - who is married to Princess Anne's daughter Zara.

Haskell joked: "I don't know how to say this but Tinds is a proper brown-nose around the royals."

The 38-year-old has also spoken about his own relationships, making at number of digs at his ex Chloe Madeley.

The former couple, who share a daughter called Bodhi, confirmed the end of their marriage in October.

It came soon after pictures emerged of Haskell parting in Ibiza, where he had a gig as a DJ, and then chatting closely with a blonde PR boss.

According to MailOnline, the former rugby union player made a number of comments about his marriage to Chloe during the live version of his podcast - World Cup Afterparty: The Good, The Bad and The Ugly - in Bath on Friday.

He added: "What happens now is that whatever I say goes out of my mouth and into your own personal filters.

"I don't want you to reflect on what I say and think you're offended and then posting that up. Because I need the cash because I am about to lose half of it.

"It's true. That is if she [Chloe] can find it.

"I always forget where I put it - the Cayman Islands, Switzerland. No, at the moment I haven't got a pot to p**s in."

However, Chloe said she and her ex are still on good terms as they navigate co-parenting.

She told The Sun: "We’re all spending Christmas together and James will be there.”

And, despite the split, the mum is planning a second series of her ITV2 reality TV show A Family Affair.
