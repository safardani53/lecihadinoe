Donald Trump celebrates as car giant Ford announces mega investment in three Michigan plants

DONALD Trump roared with delight over American car giant Ford's expected decision to splash the cash on three giant car plants.

The President made resurrecting the US's manufacturing economy his main election pledge - focusing specifically on Detroit's ailing car industry.

Big announcement by Ford today. Major investment to be made in three Michigan plants. Car companies coming back to U.S. JOBS! JOBS! JOBS!

— Donald J. Trump (@realDonaldTrump) March 28, 2017

And American car giant Ford's expected announcement of three new plants in Michigan was met with euphoria by Trump.

He tweeted: "Big announcement by Ford today. Major investment to be made in three Michigan plants. Car companies coming back to U.S. JOBS! JOBS! JOBS!"

Ford is set to invest billions in its factories at the Flat Rock Assembly Plant, Michigan Assembly Plant and Romeo Engine Plant.

most read in news

Earlier this month Trump told business leaders he wanted car makers to "build new plants in Michigan and other states," Detroit News reported.

The firebrand Republican added that he would bring "thousands of jobs" to the state.

Just days after taking office he said: "In my first few days as your president, I’ve met with the leaders of some our nation’s top manufacturing companies and labour unions.

“My message was clear. We want to make things in America and we want to use American workers.

“Since my election many companies have announced they are no longer moving jobs out of our country but are instead keeping and creating jobs right here in America."

The President later added: "[I will issue] a directive to expedite permits for new infrastructure and new manufacturing plants."
