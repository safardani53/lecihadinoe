Inside the stunning home from BBCs The Nest that you can rent for 3k-a-night The Sun

YOU can stay in the plush home featured in BBC drama The Nest - if you have a spare £3,000 per night knocking about.

The luxury five-bedroom home, in Argyll, Scotland, boasts its own private beach and has incredible views of Loch Long.

The hit show, which stars Martin Compson, Mirren Mack and Sophie Rundle, tells the story of couple Dan and Emily's surrogacy bid.

However, for many, the real star was the pair's stunning waterside home.

Described as a “magical escape”, the property, named Cape Cove, can sleep up to ten people and has a hot tub as well.

The plush rural property also has plenty for nature enthusiasts to do - with exciting walking trails nearby.

The in-demand cottage is surrounded by spectacular scenery, and visitors can also take a dip in the loch if they fancy a swim.

The modern guest house is described as having an open-plan living room and a stylish kitchen, with a large double bedroom on the lower floor.

For when the weather is sunny and warm, the home also has an outdoors balcony and space for holding a barbecue.

The guest home’s description reads: “A spectacular setting, superb style and stunning views come together to create this exquisite waterside home.

“Boasting panoramic views from the welcoming living space, put your feet up and watch the world go by or enjoy a tipple at the table as the sun sets.

“Whet your appetite then wet your toes in the hot tub or stroll down to your private beach for an evening on the rocks.
