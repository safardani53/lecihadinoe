Mercedes to recall THREE MILLION diesel cars and install software update to slash emissions over tox

THREE million Mercedes diesel models across Europe will be recalled in a bid to slash toxic gases.

The €220million (£196m) voluntary recall will see drivers required to take their models to a dealer for a software update to cut nitrogen oxide emissions.

Mercedes has not announced how many UK cars are affected but some experts have claimed it could be as many as 300,000.

The recall applies to nearly all Euro 5 and Euro 6 vehicles including anything from the compact A-Class up to luxury execs like the E-Class.

Parent company Daimler, which owns Mercedes and Smart brands, said the move was to "reassure" drivers about diesel and "strengthen confidence" in its future.

The recall - free to customers - will start in the next few weeks and will continue over a "long period of time" due to the huge numbers involved.

Dr Dieter Zetsche, Daimler chief executive and head of Mercedes, said: "The public debate about diesel engines is creating uncertainty – especially for our customers.

"We have therefore decided on additional measures to reassure drivers of diesel cars and to strengthen confidence in diesel technology.

"We are convinced that diesel engines will continue to be a fixed element of the drive-system mix, not least due to their low CO2 emissions."

A new four-cylinder diesel - first launched in the E-Class last year - will be rolled out across its entire range, too.

A previous voluntary recall in March addressed concerns on 274,000 Mercedes compact cars and V-Class vans.

And in May, German investigators searched Mercedes offices over potential exhaust controls - like VW - but the manufacturer denied any such software was fitted.

VW has since come under fire for its recall with thousands of owners reporting loss of power and performance AFTER having work carried out.

Louise Ellman, Labour MP and former chair of the commons Transport Committee which investigated VW, said the Mercedes recall increased concerns about diesel further.

She said: "This reinforces the need for stricter testing and spot checks on cars.

"Drivers must be extremely worried about the implications of this recall and question whether there is any liability on the part of the manufacturer."

Mercedes has sold 100,000 models already in the UK this year - most of them diesels - with parent company Daimler enjoying a record 2016 with highest ever profits and sales.

The strong commitment to the future of diesel comes after Volvo announced it would ditch development of the toxic engines in favour of hybrids and plug-ins from 2019.

France has also announced it'll stop the sale of diesels AND petrols from 2040.

While the UK is pressing ahead with clean air zones in cities, parking surcharges for diesels and potential pay-per-mile road pricing.
