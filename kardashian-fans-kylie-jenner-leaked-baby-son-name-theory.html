Kardashian fans think Kylie Jenner accidentally leaked her baby son's name in wildest theory yet

KARDASHIAN fans think they have figured out what Kylie Jenner has called her baby boy - and claimed that the name has been obvious all along.

Taking to TikTok on Monday, one person posted a montage of some photos that Kylie shared on Instagram at the beginning of October.

The first image is the reality TV star sat on a sun lounger as her baby boy relaxed in between her legs.

Only the eight-month-old baby's legs and feet could be seen as Kylie fussed over him in the sunshine.

The TikTok user captioned the picture: "Kylie's babies name is right in front of our faces (sic)."

The second photo showed a handmade Welcome Home sign across a black garage door.

There was also a small wooden cabinet in the shot that held a pumpkin, a bunch of flowers, and a handmade card.

However, it was the tiny Sally doll from Tim Burton's movie The Nightmare Before Christmas that really had people talking.

"Do you see the SALLY SKELLINGTON doll?!" this part of the video was captioned.

Many people automatically assumed that the baby boy's name must be related to the 1993 film somehow.

In a pretty wild theory, some have claimed the child must be called Skellington after the film's main character Jack Skellington - who falls in love with Sally in the movie.

"Skelly baby," one person commented, while another simply said: "Skellington."

A third person wrote: "Jack Skellington," while several others claimed it could be Jack because Travis Scott's real name is Jacques.

"Jack makes sense because it’s literally just another version of his dad’s name Jacques," someone commented.

Another said: "Jack like daddy." And a third person added: "I feel like Jack makes the most sense… like cactus Jack."

Making another reference to The Nightmare Before Christmas, someone else joked: "Why is everyone saying Jack it’s clearly Sandy Claws," while a different person mocked: "His name is Tim Burton."

KRISTAN CONFUSION

Earlier this month, The U.S. Sun exclusively revealed the truth behind online speculation that her son is actually called Kristan.

Fans believed they'd finally nailed it when it emerged that the name Kristan Wolf Enterprises had been trademarked.

However, that trademark is not related to the Kardashian-Jenner clan at all.

Kristan Wolf Enterprises is operating under the name Heraldo Farrell, who owns other trademarks such as Multiverse of Madness.

This string of businesses has no ties to the Kardashian-Jenner trademark attorney, as the family typically files with their attorney of record Jennifer Ko Craft.

An online internet theory speculating that Kylie's baby name could be Kristan was confused with the fact that Kris Jenner did actually file a trademark under the same name - but for caftans.

The U.S. Sun previously revealed Kris Jenner filed a trademark for Kristan on February 25, 2022 - shortly after Kylie gave birth to her son.

According to the United States Trademark and Patent Office, the mark covers: “Tops as clothing, bottoms as clothing, caftans and loungewear.”

The name of the company appears to be a combination of Kris’ birth name, Kristen, and caftan, a clothing item The Kardashians star often wears.
