Theo Pinson From Tar Heel Champ To NBA Pro [2023 Update]

An undrafted free agent of the 2018 NBA draft, Theo Pinson now plays as a shooting guard for the Dallas Mavericks.

The Mavs tested him with 10-day contracts & a two-way contract before signing him on a standard deal in mid-2022. Earlier, he had NBA stints with the Brooklyn Nets & the New York Knicks.

After the 2022-23 season ends, Pinson becomes an unrestricted free agent. So, what lies ahead for this basketball player?

The University of North Carolina attendant was a member of the varsity basketball and led the Tar Heels to the 2017 NCAA Championship.

His ability to play several positions in a sport is quite remarkable. His defense talents are outstanding, and he has a tremendous ability to confuse the defenders.

Theo has a cheery attitude and enjoys having fun and making others laugh with his amusing behaviors from time to time.

Early Life, Family & Education

Theo was born in Greensboro, North Carolina, to parents Barbara Pinson and Theo Pinson on November 5, 1995.

He has a younger sister named Brianna Pinson. An attendant of North Carolina Central Univesity, Brianna is a certified lash technician & runs her lash business Bri LashN’ Out.

While growing up, Theo Pinson enjoyed playing football & was a quarterback until he was in the eighth grade.

Later, Theo realized his potential for the hoops & got passionate about it. There is no denying that he has always enjoyed athletic activities since childhood.

High School Career

Theo went to Wesleyan Christian Academy, where he played basketball & was named McDonald All-American as a senior.

Then, in the summer of 2021, he was named to the USA Basketball U16 team that won a gold medal. There he would team up with Justin Jackson, his future Tar Heels teammate & now an NBA player.

College Career

Theo spent four years at the University of North Carolina from 2014 to 2018.

In his freshman year, after breaking the fifth metatarsal bone, Pinson missed 14 games of his rookie season. Yet, he was fourth on the team (tops off the bench) in assists and rebounds at the time of his injury in January 2014.

He fully recovered from his injuries during his sophomore season.

Upon recovery, as a nonstarter, Pinson scored 100 assists in 2015, the second-most in the college’s franchise since Quentin Thomas with 113 assists in 2008.

But again, in his junior year, he could not play in 16 games due to the same injury in his right foot. He still managed to average 6.1 points, 4.6 rebounds & 3.7 assists per game.

As a senior, Pinson scored seven double-doubles and averaged 10.3 points, 6.5 rebounds, and 5.1 assists in 29.7 minutes per game.

He led his college team to the 2017 NCAA Championship title, defeating Gonzaga Bulldogs.

One of the reasons the Tar Heels made it to the National Championship was Theo’s outstanding leadership, defense, and passing abilities.

During his college years, Theo majored in Communication studies, and his motivation was to have the complete skills of Kobe Bryant on a basketball court.

Professional Career

Brooklyn Nets

After going undrafted in the 2018 NBA draft, Pinson’s way to the NBA came through the Summer League, where he played for the Nets, after signing a training camp contract on August 6, 2018.

Theo averaged 11.2 points, 4.0 rebounds, and 2.2 assists from his five summer league games.

After signing a two-way deal with the Brooklyn Nets, his NBA regular season debut came on October 20, 2018, in a 132-112 loss to the Indiana Pacers.

Nearing the end of his rookie season, he signed a Rest-of-Season deal with the Nets in April 2019.

On July 8, 2019, Theo signed a one-year contract with the Nets & played in the 2019-20 season. Due to a teammate’s injury, he was allowed to play for more extended periods that season.

Moreover, Pinson also showcased his expertise in scoring with the G League affiliate and made 32 points in a 117-110 victory over the Wisconsin Herd on February 4, 2020.

On June 23, 2020, he was waived by the Nets.

New York Knicks

After three days of waiver from the Nets, Theo was claimed by the New York Knicks.

However, the Knicks refused to exercise the team option & instead signed him on a two-way deal on November 29, 2020.

Maine Celtics

On October 23, 2021, Theo signed with the Maine Celtics as a G League affiliate player but was waived at the end of training camp.

During his games with the Celtics, Pinson managed to make 16.4 points, 4.6 rebounds, and 4.4 assists per game.

Dallas Mavericks

Pinson is now with the Dallas Mavericks, with whom he signed a 10-day contract on December 20, 2021, followed by another 10-day contract 11 days later, and a two-way contract in January 2022.

Later, on July 1, 2022, Pinson re-signed with the Maverick on a one-year deal.

Fines & Controversy

In 2022, the Mavericks were fined $100k for violating bench decorum rules following Game 2 of the Western Conference finals series with the Warriors due to Pinson.

Theo, who was ruled out since Feb. 2022 due to the fracture of his right pinkie finger, was benched for that game too.

It started during game two of the Western Conference finals (Warriors vs Mavericks) when Curry from the Warriors threw the ball at Theo Pinson, confusing him as his team member because of his white shirt.

A referee asked Theo to change the t-shirt during the pregame warmups to avoid a similar commotion in Game 3.

Even after the affirmative reaction from Theo, he was still seen wearing the same t-shirt throughout the game, which eventually resulted in a heavy fine.
