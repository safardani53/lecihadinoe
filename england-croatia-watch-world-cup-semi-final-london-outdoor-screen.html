Where to watch England vs Croatia TODAY - London's best outdoor screen venues for World Cup semi-fin

AFTER a promising start and a convincing quarter-final win against Sweden, England fans are in high spirits, believing it is well and truly coming home.

So why not soak up the atmosphere and celebrate in the sunshine TONIGHT when the Three Lions take on Croatia? We've got you covered...

Where can I watch England vs Croatia outside in London?

There are plenty of places showing the England vs Croatia match in London this evening.

But if you're trying to tie in lapping up the sunshine with watching football, there are a few places offering outdoor screens.

From Fulham to Shoreditch, you're sure to find something nearby.

So sit back, relax with a drink in the warm weather and cheer England on in the semi-finals at one of these outdoor screens.

South London venues

Flat Iron Square, London Bridge

A massive outdoor screen will show the game at Flat Iron Square this Wednesday.

Presale tickets went on sale Sunday, 8 July at 5pm, but some tickets will be available on the door.

Fulham Beach Club, Fulham

Previously known as Neverland, this place is set out like a beach club in the middle of London and it's certainly the most pretty place we know to watch the England match.

You can even play a spot of Shuffleboard or ping pong at half time.

Central London venues

Hyde Park

As many as 30,000 lucky Brits who entered a ballot, which closed at 8am July 10, won free tickets to watch the Croatia v England's semi-final match.

The Bud Boat, The Thames

What could be more fun than watching the England match on an outdoor screen on a moving Budweiser boat down the Thames?

You'll need tickets in advance and you'll be on board for five hours.

Tropicana Beach Club, Covent Garden

At Club Tropicana, Brits will be treated to happy hour deals throughout the match.

The tropical themed venue also boasts surround sound and live entertainment.

Hotel Mundial, Shoreditch

Mundial have their own pop-up screening for every single match. The venue boasts screens from Google, and drinks from Frontier Lager bar.

North London venues

The Faltering Fullback, Finsbury Park

This quirky pub in Finsbury Park offers a great vibe and plenty of outdoor seating.

If it does fill up in the pub garden, however, they do have big screens inside for footie fans.

Camden Market, Camden

Watch the World Cup game at this outdoor screen in the heart of Camden.

Pizzas with tastes from around the world will keep you going but there are just 50 deckchairs for seating.

Fest Camden, Camden

A flower-adorned terrace with big screens - it's boisterous meets beauty at this hotspot.

You'll find it in The Stables Market in Camden.

East London venues

Shoreditch Boxpark, Shoreditch

Like Croydon, Shoreditch's Boxpark is all ticket tonight and sold out.

The Water Poet, Liverpool Street

This pub has nine screens throughout, with several in the huge garden.

A huge cinema screen is located in the cellar bar, where tables and private areas will be available for booking.

The Enchanted Garden at The Last Days of Shoreditch

This venue installed one of the largest screens in London just to screen the World Cup games, and you may not want to miss out.

Fans will be offered a huge selection of street food, cocktails and craft beer bars from as early as 10am.

Skylight, Shadwell

Lucky guests will be able to enjoy London's views from this rooftop bar, while watching the final games.

You will be able to pre-order tables, food and drinks in advance, so you will not be disturbed during the match.

Pitch Stratford, Stratford

For Â£5 fans will be treated to HD TVs, bountiful food and drink on offer.

West London venues

The Waterway, Warwick Avenue

This place in Maida Vale has beers, a BBQ and canal-side views - as well as the game on show of course.

The terrace is the spot to head for if you want to be outside.

Ealing Park Tavern, Northfields

At this great pub you can watch the match in the sunshine on a projector screen.

Cool cabanas are available to hire too.

Ealing Park Tavern, South Ealing

This pub will be screening the matches outdoors, so fans will be able to enjoy the booze in the sunshine.

What time does the match start?

The England vs Croatia semi-final match kicks off at 7pm on Wednesday, July 11.

What channel will it be on if I'm staying at home?

Coverage begins at 6pm on ITV.

You can also stream the match on ITV Hub.

Highlights from the game will be shown at 10.45pm on BBC One.
