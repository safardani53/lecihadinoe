Michael Levin dead at 90: Beloved actor best known for his role on Ryans Hope dies after years on

AN actor best known for his role in the daytime soap Ryan's Hope has died, his family confirmed today.

Michael Levin, who played passionate reporter Jack Fenelli on the show for all 13 seasons, passed away from natural causes. He was 90.

The actor died at Northern Westchester Hospital in New York, his son Jason Levin told The Hollywood Reporter.

Other than his performances on the screen, Levin was also known for his appearances on the stage.

He starred in The Royal Hunt of the Sun on Broadway in 1965.

The soap legend also performed in three 1970 plays including Tennessee Williams’ Camino Real with Al Pacino, Sam Shepard’s Operation Sidewinder, and Bertolt Brecht’s The Good Woman of Setzuan.

Tributes have started to roll out on social media as people remember the actor's iconic moments.

"#RIP Michael Levin; as the fiery Jack Fenelli, he spend 13-plus years on 'Ryan's Hope,' on the first episode of the ABC soap as well as the last," someone tweeted.

"He and @TheKateMulgrew (especially when she was a ghost) made for a dynamic daytime couple back in the day!"

Ryan's Hope was an ABC soap opera that ran from 1975 to 1989.

The show follows the struggles of a large Irish-American family in Manhattan who frequent a bar across from Riverside Hospital.

He and Kate Mulgrew, who played TV news reporter Mary, Mary, made for one of the most beloved couples on daytime TV.

Levin appeared on 1,074 episodes of the show, making him one of the most present characters.

He was nominated for a Daytime Emmy for outstanding actor in a daytime drama series for three years straight for his role.

A Minneapolis native, Levin served in the U.S. Navy for two years before attending the University of Minnesota.

After school, he moved to California where he took acting classes alongside legend Jack Nicholson and later worked at Minneapolis' Guthrie Theater.

He eventually moved to New York where he continued to act on the stage before he got the job with Ryan's Hope.

Levin said landing a commercial gig as a spokesperson for an Italian airline helped to get him the role as the Italian American reporter.

He is survived by his wife Elizabeth, their two sons Jason and Aaron, and grandchildren Veronica and Nico.
